Fallacies Once in my youth my browser found its way to the Wikipedia page for logical fallacies, and
like a child set free in a candy store my mind gobbled up all the categorizations it could. It was
as if someone had put words to the inherent truths lurking within me, and finally there were simple
expressions for complex topics. Saying something about a person to discount their argument became an
"ad hominem", and usage of emotion to distort the facts and logic of a situation became an "appeal
to emotion". For some time these fallacies became tools which wore down unreason and created room
for my intellect to flourish. It was so easy to tear down erroneous reasoning, and only the highest
standard of reason was allowed into my internal ivory tower. Oh, how I was wrong.

I have come to realise now how deeply flawed this approach to life was. I was walking through life
with blinders on, staring directly ahead and cutting down anything which didn't fit perfectly into
my box of reason. I was open to ideas, and my mind wasn't closed, but it was extremely restricted
and limited in scope. What has changed since then, apart from moving continents and experiencing
another culture, was seven years of intensive therapy involving cognitive behavioural therapy,
dialectical behavioural therapy, and various other modalities. It was with the help of several very
skilled therapists that my perspective finally shifted and the error of my ways was revealed. Other
people are not using fallacious reasoning because they want to trick me, or because they want to see
the world a particular way, they are simply doing their best and sometimes need to choose hope over
despair and pure reason.

In some ways, this body is no longer inhabited by the person who came before me. Where he saw
errors, my new soul sees a hand reaching out for help. Where he saw fallacy, my new mind sees trial
and error. Where he saw emotional reasoning, my new soul hears humanity. We are not logical beings,
and we will never live in a world governed by logic, because human beings simply do not care. We
aren't here to investigate axioms, probe the depths of logic, and construct flawless world views, we
are here but pure coincidence and for the most part are making sense of the world as it presents
itself to us. We find people to love, we use hope to get through difficult times, and we smile when
we feel the touch of another. At our core we are emotional beings who have found the gift of reason
within us, and we must embrace both sides of ourselves to achieve any semblance of sanity.

It's been approximately two decades now since my first foray into structured reason and logic, and
it's been a wild ride.
