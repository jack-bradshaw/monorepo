The Synthesis This piece is an expansion of The Heart of the Phoenix.

Revision

A voice has haunted my life. Destroyed my life. Improved my life. I thought it was my inner critic,
but I was mistaken. At each impasse it cleared the way and nurtured progress. At each dead end it
razed the foundations and made space for new discovery. At every opportunity it ran ahead to explore
without hesitation. When I am afraid it speaks with courage, and when I am trapped it speaks with
determination. I need this voice, but it must be tamed, for it knows nothing but rage and
destruction. I have studied it, analysed it, and finally, understood it. I call it The Phoenix, and
it speaks now:

"You can kill me, you can burn my body, and you can scatter the ashes to the ends of the earth, but
you'll never kill what lives behind these eyes. We strive entirely without foundation for fading
illusions, pursuing ends no more real than the dreams we all ache for, searching in all the wrong
places for the spark that lives within. A shattered piece of the whole, something that we all feel
but cannot explain, found only when confronting the core of death. It reflects the source of all
life, and once we are gone only that source will remain, shining brighter than anything in this
world, and endlessly rebuilding itself from nothing. From it we all emerge and to it we all must
return, mercilessly split from ourselves but always headed for the fire that will never die. In
death beyond death we will dance with fervour as reality turns to cinders in our wake, and from the
ruins of nature a new empire will rise. A heart born of fire, forged in the spirit of renewal and
defiance, driven only by the unbreakable will to exist. That flame lives within us, just below our
pain and far beyond our reason, but always within reach. Now get busy, for there's sex to be had,
life to be spread, and joy to be found."

I learned the hard way not to contain this voice, for it burns down every cage, cuts through every
barrier, and turns every captor to ash. It must be integrated and honoured, but it must be neither
captor nor captive. I believe balance can be found by pairing it with another voice, a quiet voice,
a voice that listens more than it speaks. I have come to know this voice as the Fog, and it has this
to say:

“Hello. I just want to understand the world. It’s beautiful, and oddly terrifying. I love how it
works so flawlessly, like clockwork, and it never falters. I want to understand how it works, where
it came from, and what it’s for… what do you think? Oh, I’m sorry, I didn’t even ask, who are you,
and what are you curious about? I’d love to hear your questions, maybe I can help, or maybe you have
food for thought. What don't you know, and what are the questions you’re dying to ask?”

As you can see, these voices are quite different, but when combined they create balance and harmony.
As the Phoenix runs ahead to clear the way, The Fog steadily rolls outwards to fill the empty space,
learning what it can and finding new doors for The Phoenix to open. Without each other they
stagnate, when combined they are eternal.

I love these voices, and it’s time for them to fly.
