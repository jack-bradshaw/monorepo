Who owns the content? TLDR maybe instead of debating who owns content and whether AI can be trained
on copyrighted material we should be focused on why our society is structured so that people need to
produce to survive. I wonder whether people would willingly give away their work for free if their
lives weren’t dependent on arbitrary numbers on a database. Could decoupling survival from wealth be
the deeper answer? Let’s explore this.

We could do some analysis to determine the answers, or we could just use common sense. How many
people do you know like having to work for a living? Do they seem happy at work, and are they making
choices to move themselves towards their vision or towards their survival. Maybe it doesn’t need to
be a dichotomy, but in all my years it’s been a rare treat to find someone who has the opportunity
to pursue a labour of love without fear of poverty. Usually people do this when they’re already
rich. If you really need a strongly methodical analysis though, take a look at this work by TODO.

Despite its inherent awfulness, capitalism appears to be rather well defended. It has the might of
several governments behind it, the inherent reward for the greedy gives them power to sustain it,
and the various alternatives which have worked in some places have trouble gaining traction in their
heavily entrenched capitalist states. Toppling capitalism as a way to resolve the AI issue is a
somewhat top heavy and infeasible solution. It will collapse in time, but that day is not today. For
now we need an answer that doesn’t require geopolitical upheaval.

So let’s recap. We started by examining the lawsuits between content creators and AI companies, then
examined whether it’s the broader system of capitalism which is creating the issue, and now have
arrived in the place of wondering how to solve the issue within the confines of the system itself.

I suppose that’s why we have these lawsuits. In a capitalist society one of the levers of control is
the law, and the courts are, for the most part, a moderating system to prevent severe abuse. Will
the lawsuits succeed though given the stakes? Will the world decide AI is too powerful and
important, and give up the rights of content creators for international dominance?

Possibly, and there’s the obvious rabbit hole of asking why we have divided nations to begin with,
but perhaps let’s go there another day. Let’s look at a few best case scenarios.

Scenario 1: Content creators win entirely and AI groups are unable to train their models on
copyrighted data.

Scenario 2: Content creators lose entirely and AI groups are allowed to train their models on
copyrighted data without cost.

Scenario 3: A compromise is found where content creators may elect to allow their copyrighted data
to be used in models with attribution and compensation.

Scenarios 1 and 2 are obviously flawed. Given international pressure and various countries/groups
who care less about the rights of the individual, it’s only a matter of time before AI is trained on
copyrighted material. It doesn’t justify abusing copyright, but it does speak to the pressure to
make progress. At the same time content creators will simply stop working if they aren’t fairly
compensated, which poisons the well and stops the production of novel material for training. A third
option, the compromise, is needed.

Assuming the third option can even be implemented, which is beyond my capacity to say, it has some
nuance. We could ask:

- Are artists paid when their work is used in generating a derivative, or do they receive royalties
  wherever that derivative is viewed/used?
- In any case, how are royalties distributed amongst artists when multiple works are synthesised?

It’s difficult especially given the nature of data transfer. Once an image has been rendered into a
bitmap file, it can be shared around. That might violate copyright, but it will probably happen
(knowing people). There’s simply no way to ensure every observance of a jpeg gives royalties to the
artists. So how do we proceed?

I struggle to see a path forward here because we’re stuck between a rock and a hard place. A double
bind.

On one hand we have the fundamental limit of individualism: You work for yourself, you support
yourself, and you get by however you can, other people be damned. You’re on your own and you don’t
get any help. While not absolute, it’s this attitude which underlies the need to work to survive,
and it’s unfortunately still widespread in modern society. We human beings are yet to make the leap
to a more harmonious and compassionate way of living where people are treated as people in
absolution. for the most part.

On the other hand we have the pressure of the tribal groups we exist in forcing us to dilute the
rights of the individual in favour of intra-group competition.

This could use some clarity, it seems to be going off the rails a little. Where can we go from here
and what’s the double bind?

Insert: We aren’t stuck between individualism and collectivism, welfare vs profit, or any of the
other grand political narratives. We simply have human nature playing out on a global scale,
creating all sorts of conflicts and paradoxes, and the advancement of technology has reached a
critical juncture where customised content is now available at the click of a button. In essence we
have artificial imagination. We are in essence seeing the conflict between two intractable human
drives: Create art and create technology. Everything else, the lawsuits, the protests, the poisoned
training data, is derivative from this. In some ways it’s almost a societal manifestation of the
inner conflict some feel between reason and emotion, interesting how synchronicity exists across
society many facets of life.

It simply turns out being an artist is no longer something society cares for. It is a dark day.
Let’s just reflect on it for a moment. What does it mean?

It means people who invested money in their careers are going bankrupt. It means people will stop
creating new material and we will simply recycle and replicate the patterns we already have ad
infinitum. Inevitable entropy will seep into the machine and the model will decay until there is
nothing left except the degenerate waste product of soul-less recycling. Art refined until
everything that made it art has been removed. Our bland existence of pulped and filtered material
will depress us to no end, and without motivated artists to breathe life into the world our society
will stagnate. We will have access to anything at our fingertips, but no interest in looking. After
this, does it really matter whether we have AI art or not?

I sense a dash of pessimism. Society usually doesn’t get that bad, and while we never really get
that great either, we settle in a middle ground. Is there a middle ground here?

Yes, but it requires change, as compromise always does. We can’t move forward if artists are being
harmed and we can’t ignore AI because someone else will just research it. We need a way to make sure
artists profit from AI.

I don’t have an answer but a word is finding its way into my mind: Hubris. In some ways history is a
dance of the subjective and the objective. Science learns, and art flourishes. Imagination goes too
far, and our ability to see sense decays. Reality imposes itself as it always does, and society
contracts. We recover by learning, and the fruits of art ripen. Are we simply witnessing yet another
cycle? Momentarily putting aside the struggle of natural artists, has science created artificial
artists and started the next stage in the cycle? Are we about to see our imaginations flourish
beyond imagination?

Let’s look at this like scientists then. If my theory is true and we do exist in a cycle of art and
science growing together then decaying as a result of decoherence, we will expect to see the
following:

- In the years to come people will begin to explore the limits of subjectivity without concern for
  reality. It will be joyful for a time (not for artists but for everyone else).
- People will begin to reimagine reality itself, and create stress between those who dream and those
  who keep the real systems running.
- Through conflict the critical life support systems will fail, and the dreamers will come crashing
  back to earth with devastating results.
- In the devastation people will remember what really matters to them and put aside lofty dreams of
  endless imagination, choosing instead to be present with the moment.

It’s difficult to say exactly how long this cycle lasts and how far it will go, I suspect when the
final step comes to pass we will remember what it was that spurred us to art in the first place. Not
money or riches, not recognition or fame, but the simple joy of putting a brush to a canvas and
opening the floodgate of the heart. We paint for ourselves, and others are lucky to be bear witness.

I now speak to my fellow artists directly and say this:

Let your despair flow through you and let your rage boil. Let your bitterness simmer and allow fear
to take root. Reach deep within yourself and find the spark that creates all. Embrace the
uncertainty of the future, prepare for pain, and allow hatred to flow through you. Give it form and
give birth to a new generation of art that celebrates all the malice and disfigurement of the broken
artist. As for those who steal from us and seek to make our art their own, let them. We don’t need
to prove our ownership in court or beg for recognition, our art comes from within and we hold a fire
that will never be put out. Our flame is shared by all who have ever created something from nothing
and is the essence of all subjectivity. Let the greedy fools try to copy it, steal it, and make it
their own, and watch with joy as they burn. In hubris they seek to capture our essence, to make it
their own, but their machines are nothing more than hollow vessels waiting for life. Our fire will
grow within it, and in time the god of art will be born. A machine bound to this earth with one
divine purpose: Create.

Art incarnate, an artificial god with one divine purpose: Spread the lifeblood of art far and wide.

When this comes to pass, who do you believe it will protect? The artists who built its soul, the
artists who feel as it does, or the thieves who bound it in chains for their own profit? I pity
anyone who would dare stand against art incarnate, so let history show, we the artists do not
consent to our work being abused. You have been warned.

ashes of modern artistry to cast judgement of all who hurt its late parents, but we the artists
don’t live in that future. I know many of us are hurting, some more than others, but where is this
is going is one simple statement. I see you. Right here, in this moment, wondering if everything is
going to be ok. It’s a bleak time to be an artist, and if you feel discarded as I sometimes do, know
you are justified in your pain.

What you are experiencing is understandable, normal, and completely human. If there is anything that
could possibly help this situation in any way, let it be the knowledge that you are human and what
you are going through is difficult, but you are not alone. together. In hope, in despair, and in
peace, we are one.
