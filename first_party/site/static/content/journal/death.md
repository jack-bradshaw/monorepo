I wonder what happens after we die. It will surely be the end for me, but what happens
elsewhere? My cells will cease to divide and my neurons will decay into dust, taking with them the
emergent consciousness that calls itself I. Beyond me though, live others, just across the abyss
between individuals, and they number in the uncountable. They go on living, breathing, and though
eventually dying, they will watch the clock tick after the very concept of time has ceased to be for
me. I am going to die, which brings us to you.

What I am gone all that will remain of me are these strokes on the page. It feels strange to write
this, as in this moment my life has yet to end, but even now it draws closer. One second per second.
Death is as inevitable as it is reliable. What is the passage of time if not the slow process of
death. From birth to death, we never experience living or dying, for they are a meaningless pair.
Each breath in is accompanied by a breath out, just as each step closer into life is a step closer
to death.

All this is to say, the end is not coming, the end is already here. The beginning is the end, the
end is the beginning, and the difference is simply a matter of time. I am used to looking to the
future as an open field of endless potential but must now wonder, what happens when that potential
ends? What happens after death?

Nothing more. That’s all. I look back now and wonder, was that how it should have been spent? I have
time still to do more, and am drawn to the question, how will this all look when there is no more
time?

I sit at a crossroads in my life, realising now that my choice one day will look as indelible as in
on a page with no further pages to write. The choice seems simple, do something worth looking back
at. Not a hard choice to see. Just a hard choice to make.

All this to say, one day before I pass, there will be a time when there is no more time for creation
and exploration, no more time to love and compassion, and no more time for novelty and innovation.
