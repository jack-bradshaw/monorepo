Trailers I usually don’t watch trailers because they often spoil the plot. I know they’re designed
to only show a fraction of the story, but it’s not hard usually to infer the broad strokes from what
they show. While that might be a reflection of tropes and highlights the unoriginal nature of some
media more than anything, it got me thinking about sampling. A trailer is usually just a sample of
clips from a show in a new order to create an appealing hook for viewers. What would life be like if
that were how it was experienced. Imagine a trailer for your life.

It doesn’t necessarily start with childhood (and it doesn’t even need to start with a piano key
either). It might start with a highlight, perhaps your wedding day, or the birth of your child. It
could also start with the day you got fired, or the death of a parent. It all depends on the story,
but the point is, it doesn’t start at the start. It starts in the middle, then jumps around. Unlike
a film though, you’re aren’t watching, you’re experiencing. You move from moment to moment, sampling
life, then you find a question.

More?

If you say yes, then you watch the film from start to end. If you say no, well who can say. Choose
another trailer, or perhaps oblivion. I don’t want to get to metaphysical with this. In any case,
what might a trailer for your life look like? The story we’ll tell ourselves about ourselves is
fairly critical to our experience of life, so this is more than just a hypothetical. How we consider
our trailer reveals a great deal about our outlook on life, and I suspect someone with depression
will imagine a very different trailer to someone in a similar situation without the overhead of that
illness.

While you’re thinking about that, two more questions for you. What would the title be, and what are
the roles in the credits? You, as yourself, and everyone else, as what exactly? Two more questions
that will reveal your view of yourself.

I am leaving this here now as there’s a dangling thread. I wonder what the trailer for the entire
universe looks like, and that prompts me to consider a physical simulation of reality where the
endpoints are fixed, big bang and heat death, but everything in between is flexible.
