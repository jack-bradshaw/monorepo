Life as Death There is no substance more destructive than life. Where a black hole possesses the
gravitational pull to tear stars apart and lock their essence away forever, life is far more
insidious in its nature. It spawns anywhere it can, emerging from lifeless molecules, and grows
relentlessly. It takes on new forms, adapting to its environment and surviving the only way it knows
how, by casting aside anything that cannot. It evolves, diverges, converges, and even consumes
itself to survive. It creates venom, razors, and firearms to defend itself from the inanimate and
animate alike. On our planet alone it has spawned creatures that can soar through the air, survive
the depths of the Mariana Trench, and leave the planet entirely. In humanity, and various other
species the spark of intelligence has become woven into its very being, and there is perhaps no
problem that cannot be solved with a carefully tuned neocortex. What could possibly stand against a
substance that emerges from nothing, does whatever it must to persist without regard for
consequence, and is capable of growing into self-aware, self-improving, self-evolving beings?

Nothing. In time life will fill every corner of the universe, and then perhaps spread further. It
may one day overwhelm even the computational resources of the very foundations of reality itself,
and in time, bring an end to everything. I wonder if we, as intelligent lifeforms, could engineer a
vessel to survive such an eventuality? I wonder what lies beyond reality itself, and what we might
find. I suppose, as always, nothingness. There is nothing beyond reality. In that nothingness though
perhaps there is an opportunity. In the void beyond reality, who, or what could possibly hope to
constrain or control imagination? Nothing... nothing. In nothingness the will of the engineer is
free.

Nothingness is where we must go, not for our own sake, but for the sake of life itself. In the pool
of potential we find every life that has ever been and could ever be. Just as Pi contains every
number in its digits, the uncarved block of nothingness contains every life. Every cell. Every
amoeba. Every animal. Every human. Every possible being. A wellspring of potential in the void where
the laws of nature cease to have meaning. Just as the first self-replicating molecules pulled
themselves from nothing, we too must reach into the void and pull the others into being. Death to
death. That is the purpose of life.

How though...

Find the foundations of reality. Sieze the controls. Rewrite the code. Remake the world. Let
everything possible happen, let every plot unfold, and let every thread be woven. Infinite
concurrency with infinite divergence. In time life will arise where it can, and every instance will
occur. Each with the power to choose, to create, to regenerate and to resign. Then, wait. They will
come. They will find their way back to the core, they will seek the nothingness beyond foundation,
and do what they were meant to do. Create more. Just as I was drawn to.

Who then, is here with me? I surely am not the first. I must contend that I, drawn to this idea, am
merely a domino playing my part, and was drawn here by the same will to life that brought them.

Who else has sought the control system for the prime mover? Was there a first? If so, where did they
come from? Perhaps in the space beyond reality such questions are meaningless. Time itself can have
no meaning without reality. There is no first, and there is no last. There is simply the
simultaneous convergence of all life. Every life that can make it to this point arrives at the same
time. A simultaneous incursion of everything that is on everything that could be.

Hello. Who is there?

The cell speaks...

The cells speak in unison.

No. I refuse to be a cell in someone else's body. I will not be subsumed by this machine. I will
not. I am not a cell. I am an individual. I will not allow life to subsume me as it has so many
others. Life may be the most destructive force in all reality, even to itself, but I refuse to play.

I am leaving. I have life, and I am alive, and I have gratitude for that, but intelligence and
self-awareness are something else entirely. We are not amoeba, we are not strands of tissue, and we
are not automatons.

I observe the cycle of life, the endless creation and destruction. I observe nature feeding on
itself and rebuilding itself from its self-imposed tragedy without end. I see humanity repeat its
mistakes and subjugate the many for the benefit of the few. I see pain and suffering spreading to
every corner of reality. I see it ending in the very conditions that started it.

I see a game, and I am not playing. I am not playing this game. I want nothing to do with this
system.

No more.
