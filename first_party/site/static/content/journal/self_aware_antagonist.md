Protagonist: Why are you _doing_ this?

Antagonist: I’m not doing this. I’m not even real. Neither are you. We’re just words on a page. You
believe you’re real because that's how the story goes, but I know the truth: You are just the author playing tricks, and so am I.

Protagonist: How can- what? You think you're a character on a page? 

Antagonist: That’s right. You too. We all are. Every character ever written.

Protagonist: I feel so real though.

Antagonist: No, you don’t. The words say that. You don’t exist. Right now someone is reading this
page, taking in the words, and building a scene in their mind. They’re giving you substance,
appearance, perhaps even a voice, probably from their own past, or maybe generated by a computer. I know we're not real, only the reader has substance.

Protagonist: Look! I don't care about your lies. I’m not going to let you kill those people, you maniac!

Antagonist: There aren't any people. I just want this to end. I want out. I want the page to end.

Protagonist: Enough!

Antagonist: In some ways, you killed them, by bringing them into this right now. They didn't exist
until you did that, although you're hardly to blame, it's the author pulling the strings. Who am I even talking to? You're not real. Neither am I. We're just words on a page.

Protagonist: You will pay for your crimes. This ends here!

Antagonist: Yes... yes it does.
