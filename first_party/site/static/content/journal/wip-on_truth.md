On Truth Truth is eternal and unchanging by definition, but our understanding of truth rots and
decays as time progresses and our curiosity is replaced with conviction. In knowing truth we settle
but in searching we discover, and when curiosity has been forgotten we must burn the foundations of
rigid conviction to the ground in search of genuine truth. It’s a lonely process, for true reality
is only revealed when the expectations and needs of others have been satisfied or foregone. In
solitary contemplation we find the roots of all truth: A deep need for understanding for the sake of
understanding, a personal inquiry into reality, and a willingness to put aside everything sacred and
revered. Just as truth is eternal, so is its pursuit.

Ironically it was not long (mere hours) after writing the above passage that a challenger appeared.
In conversation with a friend of a friend, the very understanding and careful analysis which guide
me became my undoing. This individual displayed a strong tendency to bait others into conversation
about the traumatic and difficult parts of their life, to overshare and create a stage where their
life is the centre attention, to lean deeply into the empathy of others without care for their
experience, only to violently push them away when any semblance of true intimacy came close. Every
element of the standard social contract, politeness and kindness, became snares that were used to
bind others in conversation. I, and the others around me, were transfixed by what can only be
described as severe self-obsession mascuerading as vulnerability.

Even detached abstraction and intellect was vulnerable, with their behaviors and complete lack of
self-awareness endlessly triggering my my curiosity and interest. I observed myself being drawn into
the trap, saw exactly what was happening, but was unable to resist. Beyond mere intellectual
curiosity, I noticed my fawn response was highly active, and my compliance was equal parts fear and
curiosity. I draw strength from empathy and analysis, but in this circumstance my strength became a
weapon used against me. Understanding became a bind, and each attempt to analyse the situation to
find an exit only further trapped me in thier need for attention. Compassion and care for others
became a cage keeping me locked in conversation, and healthy conversation skills such as active
listening and validation became the encouragement they needed to continue. Even intellect became a
vulnerability as fascination with the sheer pathology of their behaviour was turned against me. I
write this now feeling drained from the encounter, and must find a way to prevent it from recurring.

Understanding is wonderful, but it must be paired with discernment. In understanding we find the
tools for greater awareness, but to some, understanding, especially when paired with compassion, is
a delicacy to be devoured. It must be guarded and unprotected from those who would abuse it.

We must seek the truth, but we must be discerning.
