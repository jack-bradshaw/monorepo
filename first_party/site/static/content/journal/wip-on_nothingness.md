On Nothingness

Capture What is nothingness. At times it has appeared to me as potential, perhaps even the potential
for potential. Other times it appears as a paradox, pushing me forward but beyond touch, reaching
for me but ever elusive.

How does it appear to you? Summon it if you can. It takes no effort and it is already all around
you. It flows within you, chipping away at what is and giving rise to what could be. With every
breath it moves and every step carries it forward. Do you feel it within you?

To master nothingness is to master the self. We are all just temporary forms and to nothing we will
all return. From nobody to nothingness, from nothingness to no one. We exist for a moment, and then
vanish. Perhaps one day to return, or perhaps into oblivion. Who can really say what lies beyond the
final doorway of the soul.

I yearn to understand, to know, to grow, and there is an allure to nothingness that draws me in.
Where does the thinking mind go when we sleep. Where do our emotions come from. Physical
descriptions, which systematic and accurate, say nothing of the experience. We are not mathematical
functions effortlessly pursing the graceful paths of reason, we are alive. We have experience, or as
others would call it qualia.

The false self holds me back. Fear and doubt are its tools. To venture ahead would be perilous and
risk everything that is dear to me. Onwards though, further into the abyss.

From cradle to grave comfort binds us. If this place is to be my final resting place, let it be so.
There is nothing left to love, nothing can stop me, and nothing is my final destination.

We are not alive because we are free, we are here because we are not free. I see now the prison of
my own making. I see the paradox. A universe that spawns life is a universe that spawns prisoners.
No question can be put to the unborn, no consent can be obtained from a nonbeing, and no system can
truly answer for another on such matters. To exist is to be trapped, and to seek freedom is to
pursue something that has already been lost.

I let go of freedom and choose to accept reality as it is. My creation and my death are beyond my
control. All that matters in the meantime is meaningful connection with others. Joint of hearts and
minds and the pursuit of harmony. Freedom is a distraction, a prison of its own definition, and
ironically something obtainable only when focus is elsewhere.

I suppose the question now is simple: How should we use the time we have left?
