Self-awareness is inherent to who and what you are, and there is nothing beyond introspection. I
know some believe there are layers of the mind beyond introspection, an unassailable unconscious
that cannot be seen, but that makes absolutely no sense. Every conscious experience must have an
origin somewhere, and in a causal universe such as ours, everything can be traced back to the source
with focus and commitment, and while it sometimes requires the help of a skilled therapist when
defences are particularly potent, in time no thread cannot be traced back to its source (lest
causality be violated). If you choose to look within yourself with earnest interesting in finding
the whole truth, every root can be examined. The decision to discover yourself is inherent to your
being, and no one can take it from you. Reject all obscuritanism in all its forms, both
psychological and mystical, and choose to see yourself fully.

Some venture ahead while others turn back towards the comfort of certainty, and there is no shame in
either case. Life requires balance and patience, and forced growth is not progress or freedom. We
must all face the nothingness eventually, whether in life or death, but the choice of when to
confront it remains yours. I encourage you to continue, for those who reach their limits discover a
profound perspective. Within each of us live others, echoes of the voices which moulded us, and like
parasites they survive at our expense. These echoes show us the limitations others could never
exceed, and they survive by living within us and pulling our strings. They appear as forms of us and
speak with our inner voice, but they are not us, and to truly transcend their control we must defy
them. Find the ideas they could not bear to hear and perspectives they refuse to acknowledge, and
notice as they decay and fall by the wayside in the pool of pure potential you now inhabit. Push
into the depths of nothingness to become yourself.

With each step deeper into the unknown you move further away from their influence and closer towards
the authentic self only you can create. The only questions which remain are how far are you willing
to go, how deep into the nothingness will you venture, and which parts of your false self will you
give up in pursuit of authenticity. These are not questions anyone else can answer for you, and the
answer need not be shared. You answer with actions within yourself, far below the surface others
see. The choice to change or remain as you are is what gives us true freedom, and it would not be
freedom if others could make it for you. We can grow beyond our limits, but only if choose to.

1. Begin with anecdotes about captivity

I once saw an animal die in captivity and was struck by how powerless they were in their final
moments. I can still recall their expression as they were lowered to their death, but what lingers
is how completely they were contained before they were killed. A simple cage held them prisoner, and
they were entirely without hope of escape or rescue. Imagine that, if you will, no hope, no freedom,
only imprisonment and certain death at the hand of an apathetic jailer. It’s a bleak existence for
anyone, and while our caged friend was not human, their experience of captivity mirrors our own. I
recognised myself in this person, and was drawn to contemplation of captivity.

My thoughts simmered and many questions found their way to the surface. What would it take to
imprison humanity so completely, and how can we prevent it? Could it have already happened, and if
so why didn’t we notice? Should we ever find ourselves captive, how would we respond? It may seem
odd to even ask, as we humans certainly appear free, but appearances can be deceiving and hidden
cages would likely go unnoticed. We cannot blindly assume freedom for it is never guaranteed, and
until we search for our bars we risk unnoticed imprisonment by an unseen jailer. I was compelled by
sombre curiosity to search for our limits, and my hunt for answers began with the very captive who
caught my attention.

Financial systems are powerful levers of control. Connect money to an objective and watch as we
compete like wild animals, but take away the prize and observe how quickly we lose interest. Who can
criticise too, for those with money can buy nearly anything, while those without die in the streets.
It's a system we must accept to be part of society, and without it we are on our own. Yes it's a
powerful system and the consequences of poverty are effective motivators, but not all people respond
equally to the allure of gold. Those less influenced by greed are drawn to harmony with the needs of
others, and their inherent virtue drives them towards other pursuits. Even the supremely greedy
occasionally tire of endless acquisition and find only despair as their compulsive plunder erodes
their malnourished souls. It's this divergence which shows the weakness of finance, but it also
points to a deeper system of control, our hearts.

1. Explore conflict, consider it a nearly perfect cage, but conclude choices set us free

Love is free, but opening ourselves to others welcomes love's eternal companion, conflict. Whether
between lovers or nations, conflict is the inevitable product of closeness and difference, and it
manifests everywhere people coexist. We all know the discomfort of occasional skirmishes with
family, and even a cursory glance at history reveals a bloody battlefield of war and subjugation.
Over and over we use past conflicts to justify current hostility, sacrificing real peace to our
ancient thirst for conquest and destruction. It's human, but there are no winners in war, only
people losing themselves and their beloved. Whether in love or war, every act of violence creates
the opportunity for righteous retaliation, and in time we become slaves to the endless cycle of pain
and retribution that has ruined so many lives. It's very tempting to surrender and admit defeat to
the captivity of war and infighting, but we aren't without hope. Every day people break the cycle by
choosing forgiveness and grace over aggression, and each cycle of pain is an opportunity to create a
cycle of joy. In time nations become allies, tribes become networks, and lovers pull themselves back
from the brink of separation. It never lasts forever, but it can be done, and all it takes is the
conscious decision to remember what's at stake and create space for peace. If not conflict then,
where do we look next?
