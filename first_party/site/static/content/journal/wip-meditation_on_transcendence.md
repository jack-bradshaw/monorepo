Meditation on Transcendence

Refined

What follows is a piece which has been in development for approximately four months and is my first
published meditation. It began with a stray thought that lingered longer than usual, and has since
spawned an unending inquiry within myself. I find myself unable to put the idea down, and what
follows is not a final answer, but rather a polished version of the first iteration that worked as a
coherent piece. I hope to eventually produce a more detailed and nuanced version that goes further
and examines the difficult areas in depth, and for now please consider this piece an authentic
expression of a writer searching for their voice in the only way possible. Without further ado, I
present to you, Meditation on Transcendence.

I once saw an animal in a cage and was struck by how completely they were contained. They had no
hope of escape, and will likely spend the remainder of their life in captivity. Imagine that, if you
will. No hope, no escape, no freedom. Only lifelong imprisonment and eventual death. As my thoughts
simmered, a question found its way to the surface: How would you contain humanity so completely? It
might sound nefarious to even ask, but we need to understand our limitations before we can answer
a more benevolent question: How can we exceed them? We must ask, because appearances can be
deceiving and some cages cannot be seen, so if we truly wish to be free, then we must contemplate
our absolute limits and find ways to transcend them.

My search began with the very being who spawned the question. Animals, such as our caged friend, are
rather easy to contain with walls, gates, and fences, and while some escape on occasion, more
complex mechanisms exist for the escape artists. We humans fall victim to these same physical
barriers (with many of us subsisting in literal prisons) but given enough time we use our
intelligence to overcome even the most oppressive physical constraints. From just two questions, how
and why, we can answer any question, and our mastery of machinery has already allowed us to drill
through the earth, create megastructures, and even leave the gravity well of our planet. If not
physical boundaries, how then are we limited?

I wonder if financial systems are the answer, for one need only connect a prize to an objective to
watch us compete like wild animals. With money we can buy nearly anything and without it we die in
the streets, and all that limits us is the availability of capital and the presence of a willing
seller. Despite the power of money, not everyone responds equally to the allure of wealth, and those
less afflicted by greed eventually learn to live in harmony with the needs of others. By finding
ways to obtain and use funding in alignment with their core principles and preferred way of being,
they free themselves from the trap of material desire and find something more meaningful. Even the
supremely greedy sometimes tire of endless acquisition, and find only despair as their continued
plunder erodes their malnourished hearts. I believe our willingness to put aside wealth for others
speaks to a deeper and more fundamental means of control, far beyond anything monetary or material.

I refer, of course, to connection and love. We all crave the closeness of another in some way, be
they one or many, so could our desire for others be the means by which we are contained? If our
wallets are a chain around our neck, our beloved are our reason to persist, but while wrangled love
is a powerful motivator, its efficacy as a means of control is questionable. You need only give
someone a lover to watch them smile, but threaten that love and watch as rage takes its place,
bubbling and churning until it cannot be contained any further. In time that righteous malice will
find its way to the source of any manipulation and turn on the jailer, so to control through love is
to court death. It would seem wise, then, not to provoke that monster.

I have considered multiple cages and must consider, is the question itself the answer? We humans are
smart enough to understand and contemplate freedom, both abstractly and literally, and the pursuit
of freedom is a tale woven across history. From ancient Egypt to modern times, examples of forced
servitude and the countervailing resistance are rife. Whenever and wherever you look, you can find
humans fighting against their captors, and I must wonder, is this yearning and this striving the
prison? Has the pursuit of an exit become the very maze we seek to escape? It’s an intriguing idea,
but slavery and servitude are very real, and no one can blame a slave for seeking freedom. While
such pursuits may in time create other issues, freedom itself is better than the alternative.

Where does this leave us? If there are no walls we cannot climb and no doors we cannot unlock, what
holds us back? If there are no prisons we cannot escape and no towers we cannot scale, what keeps us
inside? If all financial controls collapse in due time and hold only limited power, what influences
our decisions? If there is no distance that can separate lovers and no limit to our willingness to
protect one another, what causes our relationships to decay? If the hunt isn’t the bind and the
search for freedom is worthwhile, what gets in our way? Maybe the question is not what, but who?

Perhaps we do this to ourselves. We humans have risen to mastery over nature, for the most part, and
in doing so have become both the jailer and the jailed. We build the walls, we build the financial
systems, and we choose love over solitude. In fairness, love is not a choice, but its pursuit is.
Our own actions are the chains which bind us, and to find freedom, we must understand what drives
us. Those answers cannot be found in the world around us, and must turn our inquiry inwards. We are
leaving the outer world behind now and looking inwards.

Secrets lie within every human, voices and systems unseen. We hear our thoughts as our own and cling
so desperately to our convictions, but rarely stop to ask ourselves where our own minds came from.
We harbour thoughts, but where is the line between guest and host drawn, and have you ever wondered
where our instructors received their instruction? Where did our teachers received their tuition, do
you think they asked themselves these same questions, and if so, what did they find? How far back, I
wonder, can we trace the pathways of our thoughts? I refer, of course, to introspection and
self-awareness.

I invite you to turn your attention on yourself and watch how you work without shame or judgement.
Follow the motions of your being from the surface to the depths, and search for something truly your
own. Take a thought, any thought, and find what came just before. Trace it back as far as you can
go, then trace below it and see where it stands. See not only where it goes and where it came from,
but what it serves and upon which it rests. Question yourself, then question your questions, and
challenge the unseen assumptions that bind you. From thoughts find feelings, from feelings find
thoughts, and between them find choices. Between these impulses you will discover decisions you are
barely aware of, and push into those decisions to find new ways of thinking. Go as deep and as wide
as you can, until every corner of your being has been examined.

As you go, bring mindfulness to your experiences but hold onto nothing. Thoughts and feelings will
come, and thoughts and feelings will go. That which is genuine and authentic will remain, and that
which belongs to others will dissipate. Loosen your grip on what is, and allow yourself to consider
what could be. Tentative consideration will reveal places you don’t want to look and your perception
will distort, but to find peace and clarity you must examine everything with courage and compassion.
Growing self-awareness and self-mastery is the journey of a lifetime, but you will eventually reach
a point where your soul bears witness to its own beauty. Venture far enough into this place and the
boundary between perception and reality will fade into nothingness.

In this place, you will see yourself and others for who we truly are. Not beings of reason or
emotion, not as separate individuals, and not as animals in search of food, but rather as currents
moving deeply within an ocean of sensation and stimulus. Here we become a dance of nature,
awareness, and something ever elusive. In the depths you will find new perspectives, see patterns in
what once was chaos, and discover new ways of existing. Your memories become places you can explore,
your perception becomes malleable, and your own life becomes a story in a novel. To dream is to
float along a river, as a leaf in a stream, but to meditate and reflect in this manner is to swim
deeply with open eyes. Beware though, for only the courageous few dare go this far, and those who do
understand why. Drinking from the source yields a perspective that threatens the safety and comfort
of the surface world, and few find themselves wanting to return to the shallows. The darkness of the
depths hides secrets, but the fruits we find may become snares, and to remain here risks our journey
becoming the very prison we seek to understand.

To move forward, allow yourself to relax into the ambiguity of the great unknown. Create space for
everything which frightens you and consider the possibilities at the edges of your perception, like
attempting to find the boundary of your vision or hear pure silence. Search something just beyond
your perception, go past comfort, and let go of certainty. Look for the paradoxes you have never
been able to understand, for in such conflicts we find the key to new understanding, and for a
conscious being there is no paradox greater than the contemplation of absolute nothingness.

Within each of us lives an endless wellspring of nothing. It’s a void without shape or form, absent
of both light and darkness. It has no beginning and no end, no edge or boundary, and no prerequisite
way of being. In this place we find no right or wrong, no spectrum of grey, and no absolute
morality. Even the freedom we sought evaporates, but no captivity replaces it. Every dichotomy loses
meaning, and all that exists is equanimity and the potential for creation. While it can be tempting
to ascribe spiritual meaning, this is not a metaphysical or mystical realm and there is no magic, we
have simply found the untapped potential deep within you. It represents everything you could be but
have not become, everything you could do but dare not consider, and everything you could learn but
haven't thought to think about. It’s not only your potential in this moment, but the potential to
transcend your limitations entirely.

Nothingness may seem nonsensical to consider nothingness as the gateway to growth, but tell me,
where were you before your birth? You were nowhere and no one, neither conscious nor unconscious,
and there are still parts of you which are yet to make the journey. They lie in waiting, longing for
you to connect and make them real, so search the nothingness for your missing parts and use your
will to bring them into being. We did it once before, so we must return to the source of ourselves
and confront our limitations if we wish to transcend them, so push forward and become one with
nothingness.

As you journey into this place you will find a cost. It hurts to become more than you are, because
sometimes we must cast off that which once had purpose to make space for new growth. I found the
nothing to be like acid to the forms of my being, but everything it wore down it replaced with
something new. As you push forward into the void each step will bring more pain, and while suffering
remains a choice forevermore, the pain of growth cannot be avoided. The weight on your soul will
build and steadily a choice will emerge. A simple binary: Forward, or no further. Not a choice to be
made once, but one we make over and over again. You can continue refining and rebuilding yourself,
or you can stop and be satisfied with who you are now.

Those who can take the pain and greet it with open arms are rewarded with nothing but empty space
for a more authentic self to fill, but there is no shame in turning back towards the comfort of the
familiar and the comfortable. We must all face the nothingness eventually, whether in life or death,
but the choice of when to confront it remains yours. It would not be freedom if others could make
the decision for you, and it would not be freedom is others could take it away. We can grow beyond
our limits, but only if we choose to.

My journey through the void led me here but there is an infinite abyss ahead of me and no sign of an
end, so perhaps it would be best to part ways for now. The only questions which remain are how far
are you willing to go, how deep into the nothingness will you venture, and which parts of your
present self will you give up in your pursuit of authenticity. These are not questions anyone else
can answer for you, and the answer need not be shared with anyone. You answer with actions deep
within yourself, and the choice to change or remain as you are is what gives us true freedom.

As we part I will leave you with one final thought. Life requires balance, and growth for the sake
of growth is not progress or freedom. We must take time to balance growth with simply being enough,
and endless growth at all costs would simply be yet another prison.
