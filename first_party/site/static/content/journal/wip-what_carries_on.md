The mind is not singular, nor is it unbreakable. Like all machines it can be damaged, disassembled,
and pieces replaced, but what carries on? When all that was a person has been replaced, what is
moving ahead. Where some view a question of identity, a la The Ship of Theseus, my question is less
who carries on, but _what_? Put aside the individual, the identity, and the ego, and simply ask,
what is the underlying system that keeps going even as pieces fall by the wayside, and how far can
it go until it becomes dust? What system could possibly have the property that it continues to exist
even as it is destroyed?

Perhaps the answer is simple. The mind does not serve the ego nor the organism, instead it serves
the fundamental biological imperative of all life: survival. Everything else is mere artifice and
aesthetic. A mind can be broken and repaired with new material, taken from others or created a new,
but it does not stop until it can go no further, for the mind is little more than an extension of
life itself.
