The Death of Taoism As a child my parents indoctrinated me with catholic religion, but at age five
it became clear belief was a choice not an obligation. I cast off their beliefs, declared myself an
atheist, and lived my life in accordance with basic common sense. I studied all branches of science,
went on to warn a degree in mechanic engineering, and worked in technology. My mind flourished free
from dogma and rigidity until a friend introduced me to philosophy, and in an attempt to gain a
comprehensive view of the topic my survey included Taoism. A religion with a formidably text.

All religious texts by their nature are parasitic. Within their pages live ideas and programs which
infect our minds when we consider them and subdue our ability to critically analyse them. I
approached such texts as adversaries, or rather subjects to be studied, and none posed a significant
threat. Taoism was an exception. I encounter it at a time of high stress when my defenses were
weaker, enough for its hooks to land. It seems to draw me in, and the more I found it the more power
it gained. It was as if the text were written to dismantle intelligent thought and reinforce
confusion. In time I found myself bordering on accepting the text not as epistemological truth, but
as a genuinely true way of being. 

I have since purged it from my mind as truth but unlike christianity which has little of value to
offer, i have retained taoism as a philosophy and perspective (though not a truth to be believed).
I am once again free of all religion, and its corrupting effects in my condition has been eradicated, but through filtration and purification,
taoism turned out to be closer to a symbiote than a parasite. i wonder whether such a symbiote or
a prosthetic could be engineered. can we engineer a text that uses the hooks and tools of religion
but is specifically designed to aid the believer, give them choice and awareness of the nature of it
(as opposed to deception as a means of control)?
