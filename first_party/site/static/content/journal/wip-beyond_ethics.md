experiencing various pathologies. It begins with avoidance of danger and attraction to reward,
followed by cohesion with society and adherence to simple rules, eventually moving on to balance
with society, consequence, and principle, before finally evolving into a generalized curiosity about
abstract representations of the good. It goes further though and is ultimately a path to a far
deeper question: Who are we? A jump, but let me explain.

Say you come to me with a task, something needing to be done, and it lands on me to help you. I am
presented with an opportunity to change the world, or to remain still and let the opportunity pass
by, but ultimately a choice has to be made. I cannot avoid that as inaction is a form of action, and
in time the decision must be made. I had no choice in this reality, it was presented to me as the
nature of decision, and so it is an imposition. I am bound by existence to either engage or
disengage, and nothing can be done to avoid this dichotomy. All other outcomes hinge on the
decision, be they help or hindrance, so in effect what has transpired is no different than any other
natural event. It's ultimately no different from air molecules impacting my skin, a simple natural
interaction between myself and the world around me with a cause-and-effect relationship. I have no
choice in my having choice, which undermines the very foundation of choice itself.
