# What Is Wrong?

I sometimes feel this entire world is wrong, not in absolution, but in the sense the systems and
institutions we have build are fundamentally broken. I feel deeply disturbed by the human species
and its dominion of this planet, but the question lingers, what is wrong? How do we establish right
from wrong?

I know many have attempted to answer this question and that human development creates the conditions
for an unfixed answer that changes as we grow older. I need an answer that doesn't rely on opinion
or evolution though. I need something objective and absolute, and perhaps, there is the answer.
Right and wrong, when considered subjectively, are a door to endless debate and no firm answers.
When considered objectively, they are clear as day. What's right is what's testable and verifiable,
what's wrong is everything else.

Take the climate for example. It's common scientific fact now that our climate is warming and as a
result the ecosystem is degrading. This is testable and has been verified by many independent
studies (TODO link). It's objective and factual because it's testable and verifiable, and yet, some
individuals exercise subjectivity to the degree of denial. Where the data provides a clear picture
of reality, they choose to see an alternative. In doing so they take what is right, and substitute
it with what is wrong. Their view of the world is wrong, in the sense that is inaccurate. It's not a
matter of morality, it's a matter of fact.

The position that right and wrong are inexorably bound to testability is not mere wordplay. Morality
is concerned with what should be, whereas fact is concerned with what is. When we begin with a
should and look at the world to sort actions, behaviours, intentions, and anything else into
categories of right and wrong, we inherently create a divergence between what is and what we desire,
whereas when we look at the world as what is without judgement, we see it clearly without
distortion. The former creates the conditions for frustration, annoyance, and disapointment, whereas
the latter creates the conditions for equanimity and actual progress (whatever the goal may be).

If we approach the next century with objectivity and repair the damage to the climate we might avoid
extinction. If we continue to delude ourselves into believing nothing is wrong, we won't be around
for much longer. It doesn't matter to the universe which happens, for we are simply an interesting
arrangement of matter on a planet revolving about a sun, however it's fairly common for humans to
desire existence over oblivion. Only by looking at the facts and changing our behaviour to align
with that is, not what we want, will we survive.

The point is, ultimately, subjective morality is not important, because the universe has
consequences for us, whether we like them or not, and we will experience those consequences. All
consequence is the result of objective reality, not subjective experience, and until we can put
aside our delusions we will continue to experience the wrath of causality.

I suppose what I really want to say is, it doesn't matter what we want, and it doesn't matter what
we believe. Our opinions are worthless and perspectives are irrelevant. Physics ticks forward
without error and it will never stop. Until we align ourselves with physical reality and act
accordingly, we will fail at any moral endeavour we seek. That is neither right now wrong, it is
simply, objective truth.
