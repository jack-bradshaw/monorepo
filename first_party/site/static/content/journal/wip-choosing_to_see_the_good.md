Choosing to see the good There are times in life where our convictions are challenged and we begin
to wonder whether we have been seeing the world wrong all along. It’s the nature of an open mind to
question the world and one’s self, and the cost of curiosity is a certain level of uncertainty that
can never be fully resolved. Settling on a final position is the death of curiosity, and to journey
onwards forever is the essence of learning. In the current political and economic climate though, we
have many reasons to look at the world pessimistically, and if you ever feel despair and
disappointment you aren’t alone. I’m here with you, and we’re in the same boat. It’s a difficult
time to be curious. Living as a human being requires choosing to believe in the good though, even
when all the evidence suggests it does not exist. Do not settle on the position of pessimism.
