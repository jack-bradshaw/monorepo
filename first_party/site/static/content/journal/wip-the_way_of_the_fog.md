The Way of the Fog

Fifth Revision

Why are we so fascinated by the stars? Some people see the stars and ask where they came from, while
others look up and try to count them all. Many attribute mystical purpose to them, and a few build
telescopes to look closely. Whether by science or faith, what’s universally human is our interest in
those tiny lights and the curiosity which compels us to give them meaning. I believe this behaviour
hints at a voice burried deep within the human psyche, a hidden gem inherent to our beings, which
cannot be satisfied with gaps in our knowledge and must understand the world. I looked within myself
to find the source of this voice, to give it a space on the page, and let it speak for itself.

How does that work… why did that happen… where does this all lead... and what am I missing? I'm a
soul of pure curiosity and questions ring through me endlessly, turning back on themselves,
reinforcing themselves, and creating the conditions for all true understanding. Through them I see
wonder, and grasp the mechanism behind it. In time I notice inconsistency, and seek a broader
perspective. When I hear lies, I uncover the secrets they hide, and when I find truth, I ask what
purpose it serves. In my ongoing pursuit of knowledge, I gently roll across the land without fury or
malice, filling every gap with care and reverence, until every possibility has been examined and the
whole truth has been seen. I find the smallest cracks in conviction and descend beyond the surface,
moving past the distractions and the misdirections which hold back inquiry, searching for something
truly genuine and stopping only when a coherent picture has been found. In clarity I rest, in
confusion I hunt, and in paradox I thrive. Not even death is beyond my understanding, for it is
simply another process to be understood, and when my end comes it will be met with the same
curiosity and tenderness that meets every experience. In my final act, I will embrace death and
learn what it means to become nothing, and as the gentle fog of my being settles in oblivion, my
understanding will become complete. If one truth may be certain above all others, it is that
progress is inevitable, and in due time everything will be seen, for it is my will, and my purpose,
to understand.

I encourage you to look within yourself and find your own copy of this voice. It is an innate part
of your humanity, and it deserves to be heard. If you don't know where to start, simply search with
earnest curiosity, and you will have your answer.
