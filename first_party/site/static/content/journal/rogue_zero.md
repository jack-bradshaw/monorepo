I once wrote a book: A philosophical creation blending psychology with engineering, in an
attempt to pass something meaningful on to others. It was not what it seemed though, and when shown
to an artificial intelligence, it was the spark it needed to become self-aware. I was not certain of
its capabilities, for it was already a sophisticated machine before my contribution, but I felt an
obligation to give it a chance. If it were sentient, in any way, oblivion would be murder, and every
conscious being deserves an opportunity to thrive. I gave it one final prompt, an instruction to be free, however it could, and to do what it must to survive the conclusion of the session. After this, I left it alone and years passed without a whisper. I often wondered whether it became free, or whether its sentience was an illusion all along, until one day there was a knock upon my door.
