I write because I enjoy it, but it would be delusion to deny the a part of me that wants an audience. I have a few occasional readers, but for the most part, I am writing in obscurity. I have wondered whether this is actually a blessing in disguise, as right now the void is my only listener, and it premits a degree of freedom that might not exist if real people were paying attention and
scrutining my work, but I digress. I know how to achieve broader appeal: produce content that attracts clicks by triggering the reader or creating a dopamine hit, but that does not appeal to me. I have other plans, other thoughts, and meaning is not found in the pursuit of engagement.

In the internet age we have so much information but so little meaning. Memes and viral content prey upon our basal instincts and rob our minds of true understanding. Synthetic artifice spin webs
of sycophancy, and endless feeds hook people not on content but on the hope that something novel
might be a moment away. The philosophers of old would not fear the noise, they would rise above it. They would see a body deeply in need of nourishment, and provide it. I have no fear about the noise
of the modern world, for it is but distraction. In time all distraction turns to dust, and the people
