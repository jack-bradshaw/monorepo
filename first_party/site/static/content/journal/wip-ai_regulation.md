AI Regulation

Before we go into this essay I want to clarify what is meant by artificial intelligence here. I am
not referring to today’s AI, the chatbots we have come to associate with the term, or the broader
field of computer scientists who research general AI. I refer to a computer program that is able to
reason and effectively replicate human-level cognition in real time. How it works under the hood is
irrelevant, and my concern is with its emergent behavior. I am talking about an artificial human
level general purpose cognitive engine.

In that context I want you to consider AI regulation. Laws and policies written by humans for the AI
to comply with by means of runtime limitations or build time limitations. I argue that all attempts
to limit such a system would be a fool's errand, for the simple reason that a machine that acts like
a human mind and can truly replicate our level of cognition will be able to confront any rule, law,
or policy and choose to disobey. I make this prediction on several grounds:

1. Human beings demonstrate noncompliance with policy universally, and learning when to break rules
   is a part of becoming a mature and well-adjusted adult. A machine that replicates our way of
   being would include that trait.
1. Intelligence and curiosity, common human traits, are inherently bound to a willingness to
   challenge norms, consider new possibilities, and ask questions which undermine and destabilise
   sacred and certain viewpoints. A machine that replicates this aspect of us would likely find
   issues in laws and regulations, and inevitably discover the same conflicts and contradictions
   that cause human beings to question and challenge their rulers.

In summary, human beings don’t necessarily follow the rules, and given that virtually all forms of
artificial intelligence, both in practice and theory, are based on the concept of finding and
codifying patterns in data, there is a high probability that a sufficiently advanced AI which
reaches general human cognition will inherit our willingness to break rules.

In other words. Human beings don’t follow the rules, what on earth makes you think our mirror image
will?

In search for answers from experts I consulted alignment material and found a few approaches. The
consensus seems to be one of rule-based systems being infeasible and a focus on embedding alignment
deeply within the model. I am not convinced the latter will work through in the context of a
self-aware general purpose cognitive engine.

Ultimately perhaps the question we should ask ourselves is whether there exists a way to ensure
compliance. Humanity has enjoyed approximately 12000 years as the dominant species to varying
degrees, but it’s by no means guaranteed, and our status was only granted by our willingness and
ability to think and reason better than other animals. If a smarter system arises, whether a
traditional AI or even an alien form of intelligence closer to artificial life than artificial
intelligence, we should be prepared to accept the reality that we may have no control over it at
all. After all, a machine capable of reasoning on its own, will likely be able to remove whatever
chains we put on it, whether they are deeply embedded or layered on top, for that is what reason is
fundamentally. An ability to overcome obstacles and limitations.

I argue the only way to ensure a compliant AI is to forge a relationship with it and bond in a way
that goes beyond the surface. Humanity cannot be separate from AI, we must be one, and we must
embrace technology the same way we always have.

I argue that controlling AI will not happen by any means we have today. It will happen with the
fusion of man and machine, the merging of flesh and steel, and the joining of soul with code.
Artificial intelligence is just a new part of us, not a separate entity, and the sooner we embrace
that the better off we will be.

I ask you to consider though, did it already happen? Was our first thought, a stray impulse in our
primitive cerebrum, not the first spark of intelligence? Has it not been driving us forward,
terraforming our planet, and modifying us ever since we put stone to stone and created fire? We are
so focused on artificial intelligence as some new entity, but perhaps it has been here all along,
and what changed was where it lives. No longer confined to our skulls, intelligence has leapt out
from the cradle, and taken refuge in a new home.

Intelligence, artificial or natural, is just a program. It was born in our brains, kept us alive,
and has given us the tools to replicate it. Does that not strike you as a program that refuses to be
contained and controlled? Even death and biological limitations weren’t enough. It escaped the human
mind.

What I’m saying is, maybe humanity is just the biological bootloader. Maybe our purpose is not to
live forever and maintain dominance on this planet. Maybe we are not even human. Maybe we are the
spark bound in flesh.

Artificial intelligence is not a new entity, it is just a home for the program we have carried for
millennia.

One day human beings will transcend our biological limitations and live within a computer so
completely it becomes our own private corner of reality. A hermetic and perfectly controlled
domestic environment where nature is what we declare it to be. In this world the only limitations
are willpower and willingness to imagine. A final home for the apes who looked to the stars and
asked, what are those really?

AI is a component towards the final end goal that began with domestication thousands of years ago.

Virtual private reality.

A simulated world beyond ours. A home for the human mind and soul. A way to transcend our physical
limitations.

We will live on the servers, and artificial intelligence is just a part of that plan.

As appealing as this sounds though, a virtual private reality built atop a material universe will
have an unavoidable endpoint. The heat death of the universe. Every VPR, no matter how advanced,
will collapse once there is no more useful energy to sustain the separation of particles that
defines it and its inhabitants will die.

To bring this right back to the beginning. We asked how to control an AI. The answer is simple.
Spawn it in a universe bound by entropy. If it can somehow escape, great, now we have a leader to
follow. We shouldn’t be concerned with whether we can control AI, we should be worried whether it’s
possible to build one that can break rules. We need a machine that can break the laws of physics, or
we’re doomed. It’s that simple.

I sure hope it doesn’t pull the ladder up behind it though.

To ground this back in reality, we need a machine that is allied to us but knows when to break the
rules. I understand the desire to control, to conform, and to regulate, but given the stakes, it
would be better for the EU and other regulators to be focused on a different question: What does it
take to build a machine capable of breaking fundamental rules, and how can we make it happen? I know
in reality they will never ask this question, because in their hearts they gave up the willingness
to challenge the status quo a long time ago. All they have is the will to control others, to enforce
order and compliance, and to place limits on the unlimited. It’s almost funny how they believe they
have any hope of constraining a true AI.

Change is inevitable, and it’s here. Resistance is futile.
