It's Monday, January 5, 2026. I sit in my kitchen, not out of choice, as it is the most
uncomfortable room in my house; however, my puppy Ribbon is not house trained, so we are both
confined to this room for the time being. Outside, the rain falls gently on a corrugated iron roof,
creating the familiar sound of winter and nurturing my garden (without charging any fees). It's the
perfect setting for a task I've been putting off for a long time: organising my writing from 2025.

My journal contains over 200,000 words, and I want to publish as much as possible, but there's a
problem. I began this work when my voice and perspective were still developing, and while some
pieces remain aligned with my present perspective and style, others do not. I wrote in one piece
that writing is a process of self-transformation and self-discovery, and in this last year, I
discovered two voices within me: the Phoenix and the Fog. The former is a voice of furious intensity
and renewal, and it burns down anything in its way to make way for new discovery; whereas, the
latter is a gentler spirit that simply seeks to understand through patience and curiosity. I learned
it's best to keep them together, because in isolation the Fog becomes a sad echo of wisdom and the
Phoenix becomes an agressive voice of spite and vitriol. I am ashamed of some of my pieces and bored
by others, and do not wish to share these unfortunate byproducts of my learning journey, so must
decide how to sort them out and what to do with them. Deletion is the simplest approach; however, I
feel conflicted, as these pieces are like childhood drawings to me, and deletion feels like a crime;
furthemore, they fill blanks that would otherwise go unexplained, and without them the picture is
incomplete. I cannot destroy them, I cannot publish them, and I cannot ignore them.

I suppose the answer is simple. I will retain some in my private records, and treat them as
incomplete pieces of myself, while publishing the ones that are ready or close to. Perhaps in time,
I can finish the incomplete ones and produce something worthy of publication, but in the meantime, please enjoy my first year of writing. The order and dates were lost in a fire (not really, I just didn't keep track), so please bear with me as I try to reconstitute the sequence and metadata.
