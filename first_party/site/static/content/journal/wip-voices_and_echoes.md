Voices and Echoes Even pages turn to ash eventually and the voices they hold pass into oblivion.
Readers may recall the words for some time, passing them on or inscribing them on new paper at risk
of modification and mutation, but it’s only a matter of time until all knowledge of the past is lost
and all voices become silent. Even humanity is finite and will eventually become dust, leaving
behind a soulless world of cause and effect. Given enough time even causality will decay as the heat
death approaches and structure dissolves into nothingness. Is there a way to write something which
lives beyond the end of everything? Not a text itself, but a will, an intention, an effect. Is there
a way, simply by publishing text, to create the change needed to survive the heat death?
