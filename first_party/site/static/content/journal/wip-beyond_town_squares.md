The certainty of social media’s inherent self-destructive tendencies has become so pronounced that
one has to wonder why it wasn’t more obvious previously. The notion that humans could exist and
thrive in one single online town square is really quite absurd. We’re simply too varied in our
beliefs and convictions, and coexistence at scale will yield ever-growing conflict.

From religion, to public policy, to scientific theory, every aspect of knowledge and opinion has
room for disagreement. The conflict of religion is as old as humanity itself, and even science has
been subject to repeated upheaval across time. We once had small forums for these disagreements,
with norms and boundaries to manage the conflict. Now we have clickbait and an economy that
prioritises emotional activation and deregulation above sense and reason. We have global town
squares where the opinions of qualified professionals are presented alongside the musings of casual
bystanders, so with all this noise, no wonder people struggle to know what’s real and form
meaningful relationships. How are we supposed to get along when common sense is meaningless and
everything is potentially outrageous?

The answer is glaringly obvious: We can’t. We simply cannot exist this way. There’s just too much we
could disagree on in a global town square, and enforcing moderation is both ineffective and highly
unethical at scale. It's little more than a Band-Aid.

So what’s the alternative?

To begin with, we need to acknowledge and elevate humanity’s tribal nature to a place of reverence.
We are tribal people, and that’s very unlikely to change. As social creatures we’re bound to form
communities with norms, in-groups and out-groups, and systems for regulating behaviour. Nothing we
can do will change this, and outsourcing it to others is neither feasible nor sensible. Nothing can
improve until we accept our nature and make peace with it.

To live together we must accept that sometimes we must live apart. That isn’t something to be shamed
or shied away from. It’s what makes us human. We are individuals, and we are groups. We are united,
and we are separate. There is no conflict or paradox here.

It’s time to move beyond a town square. It just doesn’t work.
