Problem -> Solution It’s commonplace to consider engineers as problem solving machines. Problems go
in, solutions come out. In this book I’m going to share my approach problem solving. We’ll cover
general approaches as well as more granular specifics.

General Algorithms There are three general algorithms you can run to solve problems, and I run these
constantly through my life.

Algorithm 1: EXPAND ADAPT ASSIMILATE REGENERATE Algorithm 2: WORK PRETTY FAST

These three algorithms overlap to create a problem solving approach that can handle any degree of
complexity and scale across all domains and dimensions. Let’s take them one by one.

Algorithm 1: Expand Adapt Assimilate Regenerate

I assimilated this algorithm by contemplating the ultimate nature of the Borg Collective. How they
think, what they do, and why they persist. Although fictional, they are a stark reminder of the
power of systematic and methodical precision in the face of insurmountable opposition.

The algorithm has four operations. You perform them one after another endlessly. Sometimes an
operation takes a few moments, sometimes it takes a few days.

Expand.

Expansion is quite simply exposing yourself to novelty, uncertainty, and ambiguity. Consider what
you’re comfortable with, what you know, and what you can already do. Then, find something else. It
could be a new location, technology, resource, book, knowledge, person, group, ecosystem, planet,
workspace, field, species, machine, philosophy, etc. Once you’ve identified a new area, expand into
it.

Expansion can be both literal, and figurative. You could move to a new location, learning a new
topic, buy a new stock, meet a new person, or explore within your own unconscious mind.

There’s no limit to expansion, as there is always more to learn. Even the boundaries of what you
consider expansive can be expanded, and if you ever find yourself stagnant with no more areas for
expansion then expand into new axioms and assumptions. The only limit to expansion is your
willingness and ability to search for novelty.

Adapt.

Expansion will ultimately yield the need to adapt. Whatever you were doing in the past will work to
a degree in your new environment, but there will always be some degree of challenge.

The challenge could be physical in the case of taking on a new workout routine, or it could be
intellectual in the case of taking on a new subject. It could be emotional in the case of forming a
new relationship or exploring your own psyche. It could be financial in the case of pursuing a new
investment. In any case, expansion always reveals the parts of you which were well adapted to your
former environment but don’t work in your new setting. You must adapt to this.

Adaptation can be painful.

Adaptation is sometimes trivial. I suspect expanding one neighbourhood over will not challenge most
people. Your life will continue as it was with little change. Perhaps trash day shifts but that’s
about it. Sometimes this is an indication that you could expand more, challenge yourself more, but
sometimes it’s an indication that you’re doing ok. Furthermore the purpose of this algorithm isn’t
challenge in and of itself, it’s ultimately just a way to solve a problem.
