What did we learn from Theranos? People lie? People abuse? People manipulate? No. Those are all
true, but they’re not the core message. The rise and fall of Theranos was ultimately a story about
what happens when people search for a greater meaning and fixate on their pursuit to the expense of
common sense. It’s what happens when healthy Silicon Valley cultures mutates into the Cult of
Silicon Valley.

Let’s unpack that a little. What do I mean by the cult of Silicon Valley? It’s got all the
attributes of a normal cult. Fanatical support for the leader, strong in/out group dynamics, radical
thinking, but it has something usual cults don’t have. VC backing. It’s what happens when you take
human psychology, twist it into the horror that cult like mentality, and supercharge it with a drive
to transform the world.

While it’s popular to make fun of Elizabeth Holmes for her uncomfortable stare and her unusual
voice, but let’s take a step back. The only thing we humans love more than a charismatic leader is a
villain to rally against, and Elizabeth Holmes is that enemy. She’s an easy target, a CEO who never
actually achieved her dream, and in her we find all the aspects of cult-like devotion were both
drawn to and repelled by. I see her as a lightning rod, and I see all of you drawn to her like
lightning. She simply a representation of a phenomena we are all somewhat aware of. Our willingness
to suspect reality in pursuit of more. That’s really what it all boils down to in Silicon Valley.
It’s not about changing the world. It’s not about making a difference. It’s about building a product
that takes over the market, making money, and getting out. The only people who will tell you
otherwise are the ones who have drunk much koolaid they need it to be true.
