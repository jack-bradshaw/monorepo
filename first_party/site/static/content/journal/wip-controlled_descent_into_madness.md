Sometimes before we can grow we must be broken, and sometimes before we can be sane we must allow
ourselves to come undone. Yet untempered madness is much like wildfire: Unmatched in its
transformative nature, but formidable in its ability to destroy. Beautiful, but terrifying. We need
madness to grow through calcified conviction, for it effectively burns through even the most deeply
held beliefs, but we must control it, lest it consume us entirely. A balance between suppression and
space keeps the fire alive without threatening the whole.

I call this a controlled descent into madness: An intentional and controlled release of our inner
turmoil for the purposes of learning, growing, and becoming more deeply integrated. It’s a process
that has helped me for many years and has sustained me through difficult times. We all need to be a
little mad to make sense of this world, but if the world requires madness, then let it at least be
useful.

How do you control descent into madness though? It’s complex, and will take many posts to explain,
but it begins with breathing and attention to the body. I notice my sensations and my inner world
without judgment or resistance, then slowly invite the unacceptable parts of me to come forward. I
greet them with curiosity and compassion, and make a space for us to communicate. When they are
comfortable, I let them take the reins, and for a time, I allow my healthy and unhealthy minds to
coexist. My unhealthy side in the drivers seat, and in doing so makes the mistakes it must to learn.

Several weeks pass.

There is a subtle ease to be found when letting go of certainty and embracing the unknown. Letting
go of materialism, idealism, and all other systems of philosophy and surrendering entirely to the
unknown lets the mind and heart rest for a moment. No longer thinking through possibilities, the
mind settles into a gentle curiosity, and free from striving the heart becomes at ease. It takes
practice to achieve at will, but eventually becomes as simple as putting down a cup of tea. It
requires taking on some difficult possibilities though.

If we were to let go of all certainty at once though we would surely lose access to even the
simplest of sanities. I sit in a room styled to reflect the modern telefilm viewing system, but
without certainty how can there be a chair for me to sit? I sit regardless, because practically
that’s all right. Cast aside certainty, though, and I have no way of knowing my next breath won’t be
poison. If we are to explore beyond certainty we must take a controlled descent into madness. Only
then can we hope to record and observe with our sanity in check. We are going into the void not
madly, but maddeningly slow.
