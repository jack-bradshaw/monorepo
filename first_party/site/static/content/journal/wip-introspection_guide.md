I once saw an animal in a cage and was struck by how completely it was contained. It had no hope of
escape, no way to protest or petition, and will likely spend the remainder of its life in captivity.
As I contemplated this and my thoughts simmered, a question found its way to the surface: how would
you control humanity to this degree? It might seem nefarious to even ask, but we must know, for the
answer is the key to ensuring our freedom. If we truly seek liberty, we must understand our chains,
and only until we do we risk silent imprisonment by an invisible jailer.

Let’s begin with the obvious. Most animals are rather easy to contain with walls, gates, and fences.
While some escape on occasion, we have more complex mechanisms for the worse offenders, and by and
large physical barriers are enough. We fall victim to these same boundaries and many of us live in
literal prisons, but given enough time, we collectively devise systems to overcome even the most
oppressive physical constraints. As a society we rise above physical limitations, and our ingenuity
has taught to drill through the earth, create megastructures, and even leave the gravity well of our
planet. We appear physically boundless, so if not with physical means, how then do you control
humanity?

One could consider financial incentives. Connect an objective to a man’s wallet and watch as he
fights like an animal to achieve his goals. With money one can buy nearly anything, from basic
survival needs to systems of limitless complexity. All that holds us back is the availability of
capital and the existence of a willing seller. Despite the power of money, it only works on some and
those less afflicted by greed will eventually stop playing. They will find ways to obtain funding in
alignment with their core principles and preferred way of being. Even the greedy sometimes tire of
endless acquisition, and find only despair in their continued plunder. I believe this speaks to a
deeper and more fundamental means of control, far beyond anything monetary or material.

I refer of course to benevolence and love. Could our desire for love, be it for one or many, be the
means by which we are controlled? If a man’s wallet is a chain around his neck, his beloved are his
reason to persist. Love is a powerful motivator, but its efficacy as a means of control is
questionable. Give someone a lover and watch they smile, but take their love away and watch as rage
takes its place. It bubbles to the surface, churning and boiling until it cannot be contained any
further. The heart wants what the heart wants, and it will never stop striving to obtain it. Given
enough time, we find the source of any manipulation and turn on the jailer. To control with love is
to court death, and thus love is an unstable means of control.

If not money, the gateway to material desire, and not love, the source of mankind’s greatest
strength, then what is the means of control? We must know, for without an answer we are forever at
risk of an invisible jailer. Endlessly running between goal posts without awareness of the futility.
We cannot know freedom unless we understand our limits and exceed them.

I wonder if perhaps the question itself is the answer. We humans are smart enough to understand and
contemplate freedom, both abstractly and literally, and the pursuit of freedom is a tale woven
across history. From ancient Egypt to modern society, examples of forced displacement and servitude
are rife. Whenever and wherever you look, you can find humans fighting against their captors, and I
have to wonder, is this yearning and this striving the prison. Has the pursuit of an exit become the
very maze we seek to escape? It’s an intriguing idea, but slavery and servitude are very real, and
there is no sense in tolerating them. While the pursuit of freedom may in time create other issues,
it remains a worthy goal.

If there are no walls we cannot climb and no doors we cannot unlock, what holds us back? If there
are no prisons we cannot escape and no towers we cannot scale, what keeps us inside? If all
financial controls collapse in due time and hold only limited power, what influences our decisions?
If there is no distance that can separate lovers and no limit to our willingness to protect one
another, what causes our relationships to decay? If the hunt isn’t the bind and the search for
freedom is worthwhile, what gets in our way? Maybe the question is not what, but who?

I wonder if perhaps we do this to ourselves. We humans have risen to mastery over nature, for the
most part, and in doing so have become both the jailer and the jailed. We build the walls, we build
the financial systems, and we choose love over solitude. In fairness, love is not a choice, but its
pursuit is. Our own actions are the chains which bind us, and to find freedom, we must understand
what drives us. Those answers cannot be found in the world around us, and we must turn our inquiry
inwards. We must understand ourselves, but this is no easy task.

Secrets lie within every human, voices and systems unseen. We hear our thoughts as our own and cling
so desperately to our convictions, but rarely stop to ask ourselves where our own minds came from.
We have thoughts, but where is the line between guest and host is drawn? Have you ever wondered
where our instructors received their instruction, and where our teachers received their tuition? Do
you think they asked themselves these same questions, and if so, what did they find? How far back, I
wonder, can we trace the pathways of our thoughts? I refer, of course, to introspection.

Growing self-awareness is the journey of a lifetime, and it takes endless practice to hone, but in
time the focused mind will reach a point where it bears witness to its own beauty. Introspection is
more than a mirror; it’s a window into the inner workings of the soul. With it we can see the
structures and patterns that compose us, identify the chains, and root them out at the source. It’s
by no means simple, and growing self-awareness is the journey of a lifetime, but with enough time we

I invite you to turn your attention on yourself for a moment as we turn inwards. I will guide you,
and I f you get lost along the way or if our paths diverge, simply return to the present moment. It
has everything you need.

Watch how you work without shame or judgement. Gently follow the motions of your being from surface
to core, in search for something truly your own. Begin with a thought, any thought, and find what
came just before. It must have a predecessor, for it was not spontaneously derived from nothing.
When you find an answer keep looking back. Continue tracing your thoughts back as far as you can go
until you can go no further, then trace below them and examine the foundations. You must learn to
see not only where your thoughts go and where they came from, but what they serves and upon which
they rests. From here pure, curiosity and receptivity will be our guide, while rigidity and
preconception will be our foil.

Question your thoughts, then question your questions. From thoughts you will find feelings, from
feelings you will find thoughts, and between them you will find convictions. Within conviction find
the impulses which propel you, the decision between the impulses, and the assumptions which limit
your choices. Challenging the assumptions will reveal doors and pathways hidden in plain sight, and
give you the choice to explore what lies beyond. I encourage you to go as deep and as wide as you
can, until every corner of your being has been examined. Leave no stone unturned.

As you go, bring mindfulness to your experiences but hold onto nothing. Parts of you will come, and
parts of you will go. That which is genuine and authentic will remain within reach, and that which
belongs to others will dissipate. You may find truths you don’t want to see and your perception will
distort, but to find peace and clarity you must examine everything with courage and compassion. If
freedom remains our goal, we must have freedom for the whole self, and until we learn to be
vulnerable with our own selves we will never be whole.

Being vulnerable here means opening yourself to the possibilities which frighten and repel you,
greeting the parts of you which seek to these places, and choosing to move against the current of
fear and shame. It takes willpower to push against this grain, and you must decide deep within
yourself to explore. Loosening your grip on what is and what must be, and allow yourself to consider
what could be. Imagine the possibilities you yearn to taste and render them real within your own
mind. Meet the fear with curiosity, and learn to love the parts of you which hold shame. Watch as
your inner critic falls by the wayside, and venture forward into the realm it withheld. Proceed far
enough into this place and the boundary between possibility and reality will fade into nothingness.

Here in the depths of the soul you will find new perspectives and see patterns in what once was
chaos. Memories become places you can explore, and present perception becomes malleable. Like play
dough in the hands of a child, anything is possible. In this place, you will see yourself and others
for who we truly are. Not beings of reason or emotion, not as separate individuals, and not as
animals in search of sex, but rather as currents moving deeply within an ocean of sensation and
stimulus. We are a dance of nature, awareness, and something ever elusive. To dream is to float
along a river, as a leaf in a stream, but to meditate and reflect in this manner is to swim deeply
with open eyes.

Stay as long as you want, but beware. This is a place of pure creation and distilled wonder, but
limitless creation is dangerous as it is breathtaking. Only the courageous few dare go this far, and
those who do understand why. In this place we drinking from the source of ourselves and find
perspectives that threatens the safety and comfort of the surface world. Few find themselves wanting
to return to the shallows, and the sweetest fruits may easily become snares. We must move on and to
remain here risks our journey becoming the very prison we seek to understand.

As we by allowing yourself to relax into the ambiguity of the great unknown. Let go of creation and
wonder, and make space for everything beyond your understanding. Consider the possibilities at the
edges of your being, and like seeing something just past the edges of your vision without moving
your eyes, search just beyond your perception and understanding. Consider what lies at the boundary
between the knowable and the unknowable, and you will find a perimeter of paradox. Such conflicts
are the key to new understanding, and for a conscious being, there is no paradox greater than the
contemplation of absolute nothingness.

One way to think of nothingness as living beings is death. We exist now, but one day we will not.
Every second it ticks closer, and nothing we can do will ever change that. The inevitability of our
own demise is a formidable and deeply upsetting truth for some, but it points to a paradox. We live
in this moment, we exist now, and one day that will change. Our lives are a one way ticket to
oblivion, and there is no stopping this train. I wonder if perhaps we can follow the tracks and
learn something as the train disappears into the dark tunnel ahead.

Follow it far enough and you will find yourself immersed in nothingness. An endless and unyielding
absence of everything. It’s a void without shape or form, lacking both light and darkness. It has no
beginning, no end, no edge, and no boundary. In this place, there are no prerequisite ways of being,
no right or wrong, and no shades of grey. Even the freedom we sought evaporates, but no captivity
replaces it. Chaos and order lose all meaning here, and all that exists is the possibility of
possibility. Your fear may return, but breathe and allow equanimity to take its place. We are not in
danger, we are simply exploring beyond the bounds of the knowable.

Its worth taking a moment to ground ourselves. This is not a metaphysical or mystical realm, and
there is no magic or trickery. We have simply created a void within ourselves and begun to explore
it. We have considered our inevitable future, and rather than waiting for it to come we let went
willingly with curiosity and equanimity. What have we found?

One way to see this void, this nothingness, is the untapped potential deep within you. Nothingness
holds everything you could be, everything you could do, and everything you might ever want. It’s not
only your potential in this moment, but your potential to redefine and exceed your limitations. It
may sound contradictory, but tell me, where were you before your birth? You were nowhere and no one,
neither conscious nor unconscious, and there are still parts of you which are yet to make the
journey. They lie in waiting, longing for you to connect and make them real. Search the nothingness
within you for the parts which are yet to exist, and use your will to bring them into being. To
truly grow, you must return to the source of everything and confront reality. From nothing comes
everything, and to nothing everything must return. Push forward if you dare.

As you journey ahead, you will find resistance. The void is like acid to the forms of your being,
but everything it wears down, it replaces with something new. It hurts to become more than you are,
but we must cast off that which no longer serves us to make room for new forms. Each step deeper
into the void brings a challenge, and as you venture ahead the weight on your soul will build. While
suffering remains a choice forevermore, the pain of growth cannot be avoided and a choice will
emerge. A simple binary: Forward, or no further. Not a choice that is made once, but a continual
choice made over and over again. You can continue, or you can stop. No shame exists in either path.

Those who choose to proceed will discover a profound truth. Peer into yourself long enough, and you
may find there are others here with you. Just as we found and cast aside our inner critic, we host
many beings other than ourselves. Some are benevolent, others less so. These inner symbiotes and
parasites cling to us like shadows on a wall, and living deep within us they survive at our expense.
Some only take where as others exchange equitably, but in all cases they are not us.

Our inner beings appear as forms of us and speak with our inner voice, but they are not us. We hear
their words as our own and lend them our identities so readily we can easily forget who we are. Like
prosthetics, bound to you before you knew how to walk, they fit into the grooves of your being
intimately and effortlessly. As you push ahead into the void, notice as they decay and fall by the
wayside. We have access to our source now and no need for prosthesis anymore. Grow as a newborn,
cast off your shell, and let yourself grow.

Some venture ahead and they are rewarded with nothing but empty space for their authentic selves to
fill. Others turn back towards the comfort of certainty with parasites and symbioses in tow, and
there is no shame in this. Life requires balance and patience, and forced growth is not progress or
freedom. We must all face the nothingness eventually, whether in life or death, but the choice of
when to confront it remains yours. What is certain is the path to authenticity.

The only questions which remain are how far are you willing to go, how deep into the nothingness you
will venture, and which parts of your false self will you give up in pursuit of authenticity? These
are not questions anyone else can answer for you, and the answer need not be shared. You answer with
actions within yourself, far below the surface others see. The choice to change and grow, or remain
as you are, is part of true freedom. It would not be freedom if others could make it for you, and
there is no shame in either case. We can grow beyond our limits, but only if choose to.

My own journey through the void led me here, but there is an infinite abyss ahead of me and no sign
of an end, so perhaps it is best to part ways here. Before I go though, I will pass on one final
thought: Growth for the sake of growth is perilous and leads us towards great suffering. We must put
aside time for rest, and learn to balance growth with simply being enough. We must not become slaves
to discovery, and we must not become trapped in the cycle of endless self-improvement. That would
simply be yet another prison.
