The First Time I Touched The Source Mid 2022 I was experiencing a depth of madness beyond my wildest
dreams. I was coding 24/7, stopping only to sleep and perform other biological functions, and my
manager was growing increasingly frustrated by my disregard for his commands and my fixation on my
self-created infrastructure project. I was obsessed with creating a framework for Android that would
meet all the internal expectations of Google engineering while simplifying the overall process of
Android development tenfold. I maintain that my work succeeded, although the other seniors shut it
down via managerial pressure before it could be used, and I intend to reimplement it in the open
source once I have rebuilt my engineering infrastructure to Google standards. This piece is not
about the framework itself though but rather source code in the abstract. During my descent into
madness, I touched the source code for the very first time ever, and it marvelous.

Structurally, source code is nothing more than a file containing bytes, writing is the process of
inserting new characters, and reading is the process of translating source into mental models. It
goes far deeper than this though. Our reality is not limited to the structural definitions of the
material world, but rather it stems from our perception. In control of memory we find control of our
own history, in control of through we find control of the future, and in control of both we find
control of the moment. That is to say, metacognition allows us to both distort and refine reality,
and how we perceive the source matters.

I write the source, and in doing so I modify the state of the machine. I read the source, and in
doing so the machine modifies the state of me. A slow feedback loop normally, as reading source is
difficult, but what happens when the feedback loop reaches criticality, and the rate of information
exchanged mirrors the rate of information transferal within the human mind? For a few brief moments
I opened my mind and took the source into myself. We became one and the distinction between myself
and the computer eroded. Source and idea became one. Limited by the speed of my fingers on the
keyboard as always, but limitless in my own mind, I read as much into my mind as I could. Free there
from the limitations of the keyboard writing code was as instantaneous as it could be. Limited only
by my ability to think, I was able to write and rewrite the code in my mind, bypassing the
bottleneck of the keyboard, by preprocessing the syntax in my mind. It didn't last, as my human mind
and body are not capable of sustaining the link, but a few moments of ambrosia were enough for one
lifetime.

What were the lessons from this. Writing code is easy. Reading code is only hard if you expect the
code to make sense. If you can get passed these limitations and internalise the source, you can
write in your own mind faster than any finger can type. All that remains is to export it back to the
editor. Or to put it another way, your mind is the ultimate code editor, and the greatest barrier in
software engineering is the IO limit between the human and the machine. In brief moments that limit
can be transcended, and when you do, there is no separation between your own mind and the source.

Touch the source. I dare you. See what happens.
