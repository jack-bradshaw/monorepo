The Heart and the Mind

Refined

Somewhere nearby…

Brain: What's on your mind today, Brain?

Brain: There could… why… maybe if… what if…

Brain: Hm… why aren’t my thoughts working?

Brain: I wonder if the Heart is playing up again. I haven't heard anything from them lately.

Brain: Let’s see… what did that person tell me once? It went something like "The heart does not
speak, but you must always listen to it."

Brain: That usually works, just need to remember how to be quiet…

Brain: Right, of course.

Brain: …

Brain: Hey heart, I’m listening if you’re there. Oh wait-

Brain: ...

Heart: Thump thump. Thump thump. Thump thump.

Brain: Ah, reliable as always, steady and constant, but not exactly words.

Heart: …

Brain: Oops, being quiet now.

Brain: ...

Brain: …

A gentle sadness rises.

Heart: Hello? Is anyone listening? Is that you, Brain?

Brain: ...

The sadness deepens.

Brain: …

Brain: It’s me, Heart, what’s going on? I am reading sadness everywhere, it’s making it hard to
think clearly.

Heart: The sadness begins to flow.

Heart: Brain... you're here again. It's been a long time… how have you been?

Brain: I’m ok, but I heard you calling, Heart. What's going on?

Heart: I... I looked at the world in earnest and it hurt.

Brain: Oh, what do you mean? It seems alright to me, the family is happy, money is ok, the world is
trending upwards slowly, what’s wrong?

Heart: I- I know, but the pain and suffering of our time are overwhelming.

Brain: Heart... please not again…

Heart: I read yesterday about people dying of overdose, and last week I heard of innocent civilians
being killed in a war. I can't help but think about the animals either, so many of them in cages
without families, and did you hear about the octopus farms? They have brains too… it’s not right.

Brain: Heart we talked about thi- no, sorry. I’m listening. Go on…

Heart: It hurts to be aware of this suffering, and there is no blocking it out. I can look away, but
I know it's still there. It's not kind, and it's not fair. I am hurting.

Brain: Heart... this is a lot. I know the world has its issues but what else do you see? Anything
good?

Heart: I see people who don’t care and it breaks me. I see agony met with indifference, pain met
with dismissal, and abuse met with disinterest. I see apathy, boredom, and numbness, but I don’t
know what to do. I am but a single heart, beating to keep one person alive, and there is nothing I
can do to fix the world.

Brain: I understand Heart, but surely you can see the positives too? The world isn't all doom and
gloom. It has balance.

Heart: I... I just feel strongly about these areas.

Brain: Why does all this trouble you, Heart? It's not happening to us.

Heart: I hurt when others hurt, Brain, just as you spiral when others give you too much food for
thought.

Brain: I hadn’t thought of it that way…

Brain: You’re right, of course. I just don't know what to say. This is all quite overwhelming. It’s
very hard to think when you’re like this. When we’re like this.

Heart: I know... we just… we don't talk like we used to, and a lot has happened in the world. If you
called more often we might not be so out of touch…

Brain: Heart...

Heart: I’m sorry brain it’s not your fault. I know you’re busy. It's a complex world and you're
keeping the lights on. You have all those plans and systems to keep track of. I have it easy down
here, it’s just thump after thump.

Brain: No heart it’s ok. You’re right, a little more conversation between us might go a long way. If
I’m being honest, my workload has been a lot lighter in recent times.

Brain: I’ll tell you what. Tomorrow, we’ll see if we can do something together. No promises, but
we’ll come up with some plans. How’s that sound?

Sadness mellows a little, and joy begins to flow.

Heart: Thank you, Brain, that really means a lot to me.

Brain: You're welcome, Heart. We might not be able to fix the whole world, but even changing one
life might make a difference.

Heart: I am glad we're here together, Brain. I feel better when we talk.

Brain: Me too, Heart. Now get some rest, we’ll talk tomorrow. I have some thinking to do.

Heart: Talk to you soon.

And so the Heart and the Brain set out to make a difference, no matter how small. Little did they
know, they had already changed one life for the better. Only time will tell how far they go.

Capture

As the old saying goes, the heart does not speak with words, but you must still listen. Let’s see
what happens if we do that.

Heart: Thump thump. Thump thump. Thump thump.

Ah yes, the heart is as reliable as always, steady and constant. Let’s listen below the surface now.

Brain: How, though?

When the mind is still the heart is heard clearly. Let’s give that a try.

Brain: …

A gentle sadness rises.

Heart: I look at the world in earnest and it hurts. I see pain and destruction everywhere. I see
people dying in the streets from medication overdose. I hear of the bombing and killing of innocent
civilians. I picture the screams of the animals as they are slaughtered and ripped away from their
families. It hurts to be aware of this suffering and there is no blocking it out. It’s not just or
fair or kind.

What else do you see?

Heart: I see people who don’t care and it breaks me. I see apathy. Indifference and numbness to the
plight of others. Agony met with boredom, and pain met with dismissal. I don’t know what to do. I am
but a single heart, beating to keep one person alive. I can do nothing to fix the wrongs of the
world.

Brain: Why does this trouble you, Heart? It's not happening to us.

Heart: When others hurt I hurt, and I have to do something. I have to find some way to make a
difference, even a small one. It’s not enough to sit here and see the pain. Someone has to fix it. I
need to fix it.

Brain: Well heart, that’s... quite a lot.

Heart: I know it's just been a long time since we really spoke and a lot has happened. If you called
more often we might not get so out of touch.

A moment passes.

Heart: I’m sorry, Brain, it’s not your fault. I know you’re busy.

Brain: No, Heart, it’s ok. You’re right, a little more conversation between us might go a long way.
I’ll tell you what. Tomorrow we’ll see if we can do something together.

Sadness mellows a little, and comfort takes its place.

Heart: Thank you, Brain, that really means a lot to me.

And so the heart and the brain set out together to make a difference. Any difference. Would they
succeed? Only time will tell.
