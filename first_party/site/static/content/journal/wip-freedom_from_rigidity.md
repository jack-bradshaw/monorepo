Freedom from Rigidity

Human Captive was a journey through external and internal means of control, ultimately ending in a
place of serenity and compassion. A great wellspring of serenity exists within us all and is
available if we learn to stop striving and instead accept our circumstances and present moment with
radical equanimity. It's a sensation of absolute calm and unending open-ness to the world around us,
but it opens a curious paradox. If we embrace ourselves and our environment completely with no
resistance, we find the tools necessary to make changes. How curious. When we accept the world it
accepts our desire for change. What happens next?

A state of equanimity is a state of self compassion inherently, and in such a mindset we find
everything we need to grow. Doors open within us, rooms stretch out, and we grow beyond our limits.
It's not an active process though, it's far closer to an unconscious automatic process, and leaning
into equanimity simply allows it to come to pass. If you have ever felt this you understand what
we're discussing. It's the state of mind where all your troubles and worries float away, and your
underling pain comes to the surface. In equanimity this pain is not repressed or rejected, but
allowed to integrate into the whole, and thus we become one with the parts of us that often lurk at
the corners of our mind.

In a way, practicing self-love and equanimity in this way is a form of vulnerability, except instead
of being open and genuine with others we're welcome the parts of ourselves we often ignore or hide.
We look at ourselves and ask, who would these pieces turn to if not me? No one else can force you to
become whole, and no degree of therapy can help someone who isn't willing to help themselves. We
sometimes need a push, and in my experience therapy can sometimes be that push, but ultimately we
are the ones who hold the power to connect with ourselves, and no one else can do it for us.

As pain becomes integrated into us it becomes heard, and just as couples thrive when both partners
are respected and understood, individuals flourishes when they are open to themselves. Inner
conflicts are conflicts non-the-less, and their resolution is found not by winning or solving, but
by the conflicting parties learning to understand each other. My journey through equanimity has
taught me that conflict usually resolve when one part or party courageously chooses to be the first
to put down their gripes and put the relationship ahead of the past. It's no different whether it's
between a couple or within the individual. Of course, we have a complex issue when we're both
parties in the conflict, but it's the same principle. We have to let go of our war with ourselves
and we have to start by listening to our inner pain.

In this moment pain flows through me, but suffering does not accompany it. Pain sits just behind my
eyes, just at the roots of my teeth, and it wells up in my chest. A feeling of hurt, damage, and
sorrow. A need for care and self-love. As the feeling floats to the surface, equanimity clears its
path and allows it to come naturally. It speaks, not in words, but in ancient sensations carved into
our very souls. It shows values, meaning, and purpose. It asks for change, and it allows itself to
be seen. It's a message from the self, to the self, about the self. Hurting in this manner is
complex, but deeply human.

No one can rush this process and it will take as long as it needs. What begins as a trickle may
become a great stream, or it may remain a trickle until it comes to its natural end. Each individual
is different and only you know your capacity to process pain. All that matters is letting it come
naturally and remaining accepting of it. I sometimes gently nudge it as if to say, hello, you're
doing alright. As this happens my pain rewards me with cessation of suffering.

As the pain becomes your own the boundary between you and your less integrated parts will disapate,
until there is no longer a distinction. A whole mind does not see itself and its disconnected
pieces, it sees itself spread across an expanse. It sees itself as one, despite appearances. Like a
shattered mirror, every piece is capable of reflecting the true self, and when reassembled the
broader image becomes apparent.

In time the mirror repairs itself and reveals the truth, there is nothing that needs to hurt. It was
nothing more than a story that caused all this pain, and the story keeps itself alive. My story is
different from yours, but perhaps we have something in common. If you have ever looked in the mirror
and told yourself you aren't smart enough, pretty enough, rich enough, or connected enough, then
perhaps your pain is similar to mine. Shame, is likely what we are feeling here.

Our curious process began with opening ourselves to the world and self exactly as we were, which
unearths pain without suffering. As we greet ourselves, we found pieces which were not ready to
connect. By simply listening to them, with compassion and understanding, we find that we were never
separate to begin with, and discovery it was the illusion of separation that hurts. We become whole,
and we find the pain drifting away. Never gone, for our mirror remains cracked, but no longer as
dominant or overpowering as it once way. Heard, it has completed its purpose and may now sleep.

Sleep now pain, you have been heard, and your role is done. As the pain drifts away, all that is
left is self love, equanimity, and a slightly more complete person. All that remains now is to
understand what the detached piece was holding.
