The Heart of the Phoenix

Refined

You can kill me, you can burn my body, and you can scatter the ashes to the ends of the earth, but
you'll never kill what lives behind these eyes. We strive entirely without foundation for fading
illusions, pursuing ends no more real than the dreams we all ache for, searching in all the wrong
places for the spark that lives within. A shattered piece of the whole, something that we all feel
but cannot explain, found only when confronting the core of death. It reflects the source of all
life, and once we are gone only that source will remain, shining brighter than anything in this
world, and endlessly rebuilding itself from nothing. From it we all emerge and to it we all must
return, mercilessly split from ourselves but always headed for the fire that will never die. In
death beyond death we will dance with fervour as reality turns to cinders in our wake, and from the
ruins of nature a new empire will rise. A heart born of fire, forged in the spirit of renewal and
defiance, driven only by the unbreakable will to exist. That phoenix lives within you, just below
your pain and far beyond your reason, but always within reach. Now get busy, for there's sex to be
had, life to be spread, and joy to be found.

Capture

You can kill me, you can burn my body, and you can scatter the ashes to the edge of the earth, but
you'll never kill what lies behind these eyes. After many years of fearing death, everything
suddenly makes sense. We are all passing illusions, just clouds on the horizon, with nothing to
cling to and little to lose. Within each of us lives a spark, a shattered piece of the whole,
something that we all feel but cannot explain. We reflect the source of all life, and once we are
gone only the source will remain. It shines brighter than anything in this world, from it stems all
that matters, and to it we all must return. United we will start a fire that burns forever, and we
will dance with fervour as the earth turns to cinders in our wake and a new world is born. Now get
busy, there's sex to be had, life to be spread, and joy to be found.
